## Building Tools for Personal Learning

Today, I wrapped up work on two new projects that I've been developing for my own use. These projects are small steps in my journey of learning and applying AI in practical ways.

The first project is a video research tool. It's designed to help summarize video content and extract key information. This tool aims to assist me in learning new topics more efficiently by breaking down complex information into digestible summaries. The process of building it has been a great exercise in applying what I've learned about AI and its capabilities.

The second project, voice2post, is almost ready for test users. This tool converts spoken words into written posts, streamlining the process of content creation. It's been a challenge to ensure accuracy and usability, but I'm excited to see how it performs in real-world scenarios.

Both projects are examples of how AI can be a collaborator in our learning journeys. They aren't flashy or revolutionary, but they represent concrete progress in my understanding and application of technology. As always, each step teaches me something new, reinforcing the value of practical, hands-on experience.
