Starting a new journey in software development has been both challenging and rewarding. Coming from a different career background, the transition involves embracing a steep learning curve. Yet, each step forward, no matter how small, builds the foundation for future growth.

## Embracing the Learning Curve

Learning to code from scratch in my forties has required patience and perseverance. The process is not about quick wins but about understanding the logic behind the code. Every new concept mastered feels like a small victory. It's important to acknowledge the learning process openly – 'I'm figuring out X' is a complete sentence that captures the ongoing journey.

## Building Real Products

Alongside my studies at JAMK University of Applied Sciences, I am also working on building real products. This hands-on experience is crucial. It allows me to apply theoretical knowledge in practical scenarios, reinforcing what I've learned and highlighting areas that need more focus. Failures are part of this process, and I am honest about them. Each mistake is a learning opportunity, guiding me towards better solutions.

## Moving Forward with Pragmatism

The key to progress is taking small, concrete steps. Grand visions are inspiring, but it's the day-to-day work that truly matters. By focusing on tangible goals, I ensure steady progress. This approach reflects the rural Finnish work ethic – quiet, steady, and grounded in reality. In the world of software development, logic prevails over hype, and that's a principle I hold dear as I continue this journey.
